ERÖFFNUNGSFILM
THE WINTER WITHIN
Aamir Bashir, 2022, Indien/Frankreich, 96’,
Farbe, DCP, OV/Kaschmiri-Urdu/e
CH-Premiere

Nargis sucht ihren vermissten Ehemann in der Stadt Srinagar, bis sie gezwungen ist, in ihr Dorf zurückzukehren. Dort beginnt sie zu weben, während eine bedrückende Stille die ungekennzeichneten Gräber beim Dorf umgibt. Bashir, ein berühmter indischer Schauspieler und einer der grossen poetischen Filmemacher der Gegenwart, beleuchtet in seinem Film den Kampf gegen Unterdrückung, mit dem das Volk von Kaschmir konfrontiert ist. The Winter Within gewann den New Currents Audience Award beim Busan International Film Festival Korea und den Audience Award beim Three Continents Festival Frankreich 2022. 

In Anwesenheit des Regisseurs mit Gespräch.

TUG OF WAR
Amil Shivji, 2021, Südafrika/Tansania, 90’,
Farbe, DCP, OV/Suaheli/e

Tug of War ist eine ergreifende Liebesgeschichte, die in den letzten Jahren der britischen Kolonialherrschaft auf Sansibar spielt. Der Film folgt der leidenschaftlichen Liebe zwischen Denge, einem jungen Freiheitskämpfer, und Yasmin, die sich von den Zwängen des Kolonialismus befreien wollen.

Shivji, Filmemacher und Dozent an der Universität von Daressalam, untersucht in dieser Geschichte die Barrieren von Rassenzugehörigkeit und Klasse, die durch koloniale und postkoloniale Herrschaft geschaffen wurden und werden. Tug of War hat neben anderen Preisen den Oumarou Ganda Prize for Best Fiction am Fespaco 2021 in Burkina Faso und den Best Film, Best East African Feature, Best Actor am Zanzibar International Film Festival 2022 in Tanzania erhalten.

THE RISK
Shashank Purushotham, 2023, Switzerland, 20, color, DCP, OV/English

Purushotham explores the sociopolitical beliefs of Indian IT professionals in Basel, as presented through anonymous audio interviews. In his first experimental film, the Indian actor Purushotham draws inspiration from Derek Jarman’s film Blue to offer a unique and thought-provoking perspective on the impact of Hindu nationalism on the Indian diaspora.

Through four iconic images, including those of Mahatma Gandhi, Narendra Modi, the Babri Mosque demolition in 1992, and the Gujarat riots in 2002, viewers will be able to reflect on contemporary Indian identity as perceived by the members of the diaspora community. The interviews also offer a deep dive into the participants’ experiences of life in India and their journey of integration in Switzerland. 


THE DAM
Ali Cherri, 2022, France/Sudan, 80′,
color, DCP, OV/Arabic/e
CH-Premiere

The Dam is Ali Cherri’s feature film debut, a visually stunning film shot during the Sudanese revolution. Set near the Merowe dam, Maher works in a traditional brickyard, but secretly wanders off into the desert every evening to build a mysterious mud structure. As the Sudanese people rise to claim their freedom, Maher’s creation takes on a life of its own. 

Cherri, a visual artist and filmmaker, combines film, video, sculpture, and installation to examine the construction of historical narratives. The Dam screened at the Cannes Film Festival in 2022.

«Es muss eine Revolution stattfinden», sagte Godard und stellte das Kino auf den Kopf: Das war seine Revolution. SOUND INSTALLATION 
Vortext: Cities and Memories by Rana Eid

In this work, I explore the magical underwater world and invite you to come with me on a gentle, flowing journey. Rana Eid

It is a collective piece of over 40 sound artists spread around the world, which Lebanese sound designer and filmmaker, Rana Eid brings together in her work. In it, the artists talk about their relationship with water: Marita Sbeih /Foley Artist, Sandra Tabet /Filmmaker/Sound Editor, Vanessa Kanaan /Dialog Editor and Rana Eid. Additional Voices: Max Ernst /Painter and Hazem Saghieh /Lebanese political scientist. The installation was part of the exhibition Im Tiefenrausch. Film unter Wasser, curated by Michael Kinzer at DFF – Deutsches Filminstitut & Filmmuseum in Frankfurt am Main in 2023.

WORKSHOP 
Sur-Vival of the Image

The Iraqi filmmaker Koutaiba Al-Janabi and the Syrian filmmaker Rafia Salameh use film excerpts to reflect on the film aesthetic process during the historical changes and give an insight into the creation of the cinematic works based on the questions: How does the image survive the complex historical changes, which artistic decisions are made, what are the perspectives of the filmmakers, and how do they try to address them in their current work?.  Through reflection, including practical exercises, the participants approach their cinematic working methods.

Al-Janabi is one of the cult filmmakers of the Iraqi film scene. He is a photographer and cameraman: produced two collections of his photographic work in his books, Far from Baghdad and Foreign Light. His film The Woodman won the Narratives Films award at the Seville Independent Film Festival Spain.

Rafia Salameh is a journalist and activist. She is co-founder of the Dara Thaqafia Cultural Center in Syria. After several short films, During Your Death is her first feature-length documentary.

Workshop for filmmakers and those interested in filmmaking.
Admission is free.
